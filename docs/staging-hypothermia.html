Hypothermia

4 Staging Hypothermia

Two major approaches exist to stage hypothermia, the “Classical definition”“, and the”Swiss system“. The Classic definition requires a core temperature, where the Swiss system estimates a patient’s temperature by their clinical signs.

4.1 Classic Definition

Stage Temperature
Mild 35°C - 32°C
Moderate 32°C - 28°C
Severe < 28°C

2

4.2 Swiss System

Stage Signs Estimated Temperature
I Concious, Shivering 35°C - 32°C
II Impaired Consciousness, No Shivering 32°C - 28°C
III Unconscious, No Shivering. Vital signs present <28°C
IV Apparent Death 24°C - 13.7°C
V Death as a result of irreversible hypothermia <13.7°C

2

4.2.1 Key Points

  • Two major systems exist in defining hypothermia, the Classical and the Swiss. They have significant overlap in the ranges they use to stratify hypothermia.
  • The advantage of the Swiss system is that it can be used in situations where measuring core temperature is not practical or possible
